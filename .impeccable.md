## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Fiona Zhao (Yixin Zhao) as a full-stack developer candidate. They browse on both desktop and mobile — often on phones during commutes or between meetings. They need to quickly assess technical competence, see real projects, and get a sense of professionalism. Time on site is short; first impressions matter enormously.

### Brand Personality
**Clean, Professional, Polished.** The portfolio should communicate competence and attention to detail without being flashy or over-designed. It should feel like the work of someone who cares deeply about craft — every pixel intentional, every interaction purposeful. Confidence without arrogance.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark mode with warm yellow/gold accents — refined and distinctive, not trendy
- **Typography**: Rubik Mono One (display) + Rubik (body) — keep this pairing, it's characterful
- **Anti-references**: NO generic portfolio templates (cookie-cutter card grids, identical sections). NO typical AI-generated aesthetics (cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism, neon glows). The site should look handcrafted.
- **Theme**: Dark only — commit to this fully and make it excellent
- **Accents**: Yellow/gold family as primary accent; use sparingly for maximum impact

### Design Principles
1. **Recruiter-first**: Every design decision serves the goal of making a strong professional impression quickly. Content hierarchy prioritizes what recruiters care about.
2. **Mobile-critical**: The mobile experience must be first-class, not an afterthought. Recruiters browse on phones — touch targets, readability, and performance matter.
3. **Intentional restraint**: Fewer effects done well beats many effects done adequately. Each animation, 3D element, and visual treatment must earn its place.
4. **WCAG AA accessible**: Proper contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, reduced motion preferences. Accessibility is not optional.
5. **Consistent craft**: Use design tokens consistently. No arbitrary values, no one-off styles. The system should feel cohesive from first scroll to last.
